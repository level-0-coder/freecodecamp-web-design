Elon Musk

A visionary entrepreneur, investor, and engineer

Here's Elon Musk's Success journey

  • 1971 - Born in South Africa
  • 1988 - Graduates from Pretoria Boys High School with distinctions in science and computer studies
  • 1989 to 1991 - Attends college at Queen’s University in Kingston, Ontario. Then transfers to the University of Pennsylvania; completed a BS in Economics (Wharton) and a BA with a major in physics
  • 1995 - Moves to Silicon Valley; defers graduate program in applied physics and materials science at Stanford University to join the Internet boom
  • 1999 - Sells Zip2 to Compaq, the personal computer company, for $307 million, of which $22 million went to Musk. Then forms X.com, which in 2000 morphs into PayPal
  • 2002 - eBay acquires PayPal for $1.5 billion in stock, of which $165 million goes to Musk
  • 2002 - Founds SpaceX
  • 2008 - Becomes Tesla’s CEO
  • 2012 - SpaceX becomes the first commercial vehicle to deliver a load of supplies to the International Space Station.
  • 2012 - Tesla begins deliveries of the all-electric Model S
  • 2013 - SolarCity became the second largest solar power provider
  • 2014 - Announced Open-Sourcing Tesla Technology
  • 2015 - Announced creation of OpenAI, an Artificial Intelligence company
  • 2017 - Hyperloop One announced first successful test run
  • 2017 - Purchased x.com domain from PayPal
  • 2020 - Elon Musk has topped Microsoft founder Bill Gates to become the world's second richest person
  • 2020 - SpaceX launched its first crewed flight, Demo-2, becoming the first private company to place astronauts into orbit and dock a crewed spacecraft with the ISS
  • 2022 - Elon Musk bought Twitter for $44 billion, after later trying to back out of the deal. He owns X Corp., formerly Twitter
  • 2024 - NASA awarded SpaceX an $843 million contract to deorbit the ISS at the end of its lifespan

“It is possible for ordinary people to choose to be extraordinary.” ― Elon Musk
